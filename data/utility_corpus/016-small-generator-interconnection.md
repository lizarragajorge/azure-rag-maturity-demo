# Small Generator Interconnection — Rooftop Solar and Storage FAQ

This FAQ explains the process to interconnect a customer-owned generator
rated **25 kW or less** (Tier 1) or **25 to 100 kW** (Tier 2) to the
distribution system. Larger systems and any storage system rated above
100 kW follow the full Small Generator Interconnection Procedure (SGIP).

## What can I install?

Any inverter-based generation or storage system certified to **IEEE 1547-2018**
and **UL 1741 SB** is eligible. The certified-equipment list is maintained on
the utility's interconnection website. Non-certified equipment requires an
Engineering Study at the customer's expense.

## How long does the process take?

| Tier  | Typical timeline (from complete application to PTO)          |
|-------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | 4 to 8 weeks                                                 |
| 2     | 8 to 12 weeks                                                |
| > 100 kW | 4 to 9 months, depending on study results                 |

PTO ("Permission to Operate") is the utility's written authorization to
energize the system. **Do not energize before receiving PTO.** Doing so risks
back-feed onto a de-energized line, creating a hazard for line workers, and
voids the interconnection agreement.

## What does it cost?

- **Application fee:** $50 (Tier 1) or $100 (Tier 2).
- **Witness test:** waived for inverter-based UL 1741 SB equipment at
  Tier 1; required at Tier 2.
- **Meter upgrade:** $0 for a standard AMI bi-directional meter; the
  installation cost is recovered through the customer's distribution charge.
- **System upgrades** (transformer replacement, secondary upsize) are
  determined by Engineering Study and quoted before any work commences.

## How is excess generation credited?

Under the applicable net energy metering tariff, excess kWh generated and
exported to the grid in a billing cycle are **credited at the full retail
rate** against future consumption in the same calendar year. Unused credits
expire on March 31 of the following year and are not refunded in cash. Tariff
details and any future changes are published on the utility's net-metering
information page.

## What about batteries?

Battery storage may be installed as part of the interconnection or added
later. The export limit, the operating mode (self-supply, backup-only, grid-
support), and the customer's eligibility for any storage-related tariff
program (e.g. peak-shifting credit) are documented in an addendum to the
interconnection agreement.

A **backup-only** battery — one that disconnects from the grid during an
outage and never exports — does not require any interconnection-side study
beyond the standard inverter review.

## Who do I contact?

- **Application portal:** the utility's online interconnection portal.
- **Status questions:** the customer service line, DER option.
- **Technical questions** (inverter settings, transformer back-feed): the
  interconnection-engineering mailbox listed on the application portal.
