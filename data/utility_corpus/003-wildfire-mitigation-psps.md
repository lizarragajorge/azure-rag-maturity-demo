# Wildfire Mitigation Plan — PSPS Decision Criteria

Applies to the western-region service territory where overhead distribution
and transmission lines traverse designated High Fire Threat Districts (HFTD).

## Trigger thresholds

A Public Safety Power Shutoff (PSPS) is evaluated when **three or more** of the
following occur within an HFTD zone:

- A **Red Flag Warning** issued by the National Weather Service for the
  affected forecast area.
- Sustained wind speeds of **25 mph or greater**, or gusts of **45 mph or
  greater**, at any meteorological station within 5 miles of the circuit.
- **10-hour dead-fuel moisture below 8%**, or 100-hour fuel moisture below 12%.
- **Relative humidity below 15%** for six consecutive hours.
- **Energy Release Component (ERC)** above the 90th historical percentile for
  the date.

The Wildfire Operations Center has authority to issue a Notice of De-energization
no later than **48 hours before** the forecasted onset of conditions.

## Customer notifications

Affected customers receive notifications at **T-72h, T-48h, T-24h, T-1h, and
upon de-energization**, via the channel preference recorded in CIS:
- Voice call to the primary phone of record
- SMS to all enrolled mobile numbers
- Email to the account-holder address
- In-language outreach in the primary non-English languages spoken in each
  designated census tract

Critical-facility customers — hospitals, dialysis centers, water utilities, and
911 PSAPs — receive a **direct call from the dispatch supervisor**, not an
automated notification.

## Re-energization

Lines remain de-energized until conditions fall below threshold and a daylight
visual patrol — by ground crew or by UAV equipped with high-resolution and
thermal cameras — confirms no damage. Restoration follows the priority order:
transmission backbone, distribution feeder backbones, lateral taps, then
individual services.
