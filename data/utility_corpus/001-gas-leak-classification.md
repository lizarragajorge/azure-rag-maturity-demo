# Natural Gas Leak Response — Field Classification

When a field technician arrives at a reported gas odor or audible release, the
first 10 minutes determine whether the event escalates to a public-safety
incident. This procedure governs initial classification under **49 CFR
Part 192 Subpart M** and the company's leak-management plan.

## Grade 1 — Existing or probable hazard

A Grade 1 leak requires **immediate repair or continuous action** until the
hazard is eliminated. Indicators include any of:

- A reading of **80% LEL or greater** in a confined space (basement, vault, manhole, sewer).
- Any reading inside or under a building.
- Audible blowing gas or visible damage to a service riser.
- Gas migration that has reached a building wall, service entry, or storm drain
  within 15 feet of an ignition source.

The first responder must request **dispatch escalation, evacuate occupants
within a 300-ft radius**, and remain on site until a qualified repair crew
assumes control. Do **not** operate light switches, vehicle ignition, or
two-way radios within the evacuation perimeter.

## Grade 2 — Non-hazardous at time of detection, but justifies scheduled repair

Typical Grade 2 indicators:

- Readings of **20–80% LEL** in a confined space with adequate ventilation.
- A leak at a meter set or stop box where migration is unlikely.
- A small leak on a service line in soft soil away from buildings.

Grade 2 leaks must be **re-evaluated within 6 months** and repaired within 15
months of discovery, with monthly monitoring in the interim.

## Grade 3 — Non-hazardous, low probability of becoming hazardous

A pinpoint reading of less than **20% LEL** at the leak source, with no
migration toward a structure, in stable soil conditions. Reinspect within 15
months. Document GPS coordinates and CGI readings in the GIS layer at the time
of every reinspection.

## Documentation requirements

Every leak — regardless of grade — generates a leak survey record containing
technician ID, CGI serial number and calibration date, photographs of the
above-ground evidence, and the assigned work order. Records are retained for
the life of the pipeline plus 10 years (Subpart M §192.709).
