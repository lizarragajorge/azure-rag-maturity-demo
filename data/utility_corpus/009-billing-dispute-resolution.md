# Billing Dispute Resolution — Customer Service Procedure

A customer who calls disputing a bill amount must be treated as a high-priority
contact regardless of the dispute size. This procedure ensures the contact is
resolved within commitment windows set by the state Public Utilities Commission.

## First-call handling

The CSR completes the following before ending the call:

1. Confirm the account holder using two factors: account number plus either
   service-address ZIP or last four of the SSN on file.
2. Pull the **24-month consumption history** chart and verbalize the comparison
   between the disputed billing period and the same month one year prior.
3. Identify whether the disputed period contains any of:
   - An estimated meter read (look for a flag on the bill detail line)
   - A meter exchange (AMI install, replacement)
   - A rate-class change
   - A budget billing true-up
4. If any of the above is present, **waive late fees** for the disputed period
   pending the investigation.

## When to open an investigation

Open a formal investigation if **any** of the following apply:

- The customer requests one in writing or on the recorded call.
- The disputed bill is **two times or more** the same month's prior-year usage
  with no documented cause (e.g. addition of EV charging, pool, addition).
- The meter has not had an in-person visual read in the last 12 months and the
  bills in question were estimated.

Investigations are assigned to the Billing Investigations queue with an SLA of
**10 business days** to first written response.

## Available remedies

The investigator may, with supervisor approval:

- Issue a meter accuracy test (free of charge if the meter is found out of
  tolerance; $35 if within tolerance, fee waived for income-qualified
  customers).
- Re-bill the period using the prior-year same-month average consumption.
- Issue a one-time bill adjustment of up to **$500** without further approval;
  larger adjustments require manager review.
- Place the customer on a **6-month payment plan** without down payment.

## Escalation

If the customer is not satisfied with the investigation outcome, the CSR
provides the customer with:

1. The internal Office of the President contact (the rapid-resolution
   mailbox listed on the corporate intranet).
2. The state Public Utilities Commission consumer-affairs contact.
3. A written copy of the investigation findings.

Do not characterize the regulatory escalation as a customer "going around" the
company. The Commission contact is part of the customer's lawful options and
the CSR must provide it on request.
