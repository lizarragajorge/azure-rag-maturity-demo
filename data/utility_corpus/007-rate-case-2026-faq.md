# 2026 Residential Rate Case — Customer FAQ

On **January 31, 2026**, the utility filed a request with the state Public
Utilities Commission to update residential electric rates. This FAQ explains
what the request means for your monthly bill.

## Why are rates changing?

Three drivers account for nearly all of the requested change:

1. **Grid resilience investments** — undergrounding of 38 miles of distribution
   line in wildfire-prone areas, and replacement of 4,200 aging wood poles
   identified in the 2024 inspection cycle.
2. **Generation portfolio updates** — the addition of 180 MW of new wind capacity
   under a long-term power purchase agreement, partially offset by the planned
   retirement of an aging coal unit in 2027.
3. **Inflation and labor** — increases in steel, copper, and skilled-labor
   costs that have outpaced the prior rate case assumptions.

## How much will my bill change?

The typical residential customer using **750 kWh per month** would see a bill
increase of approximately **$6.40 per month**, or about 6.1%, if the
Commission approves the full request. The Commission may approve, modify, or
deny the request following a public hearing process expected to conclude in
**Q4 2026**.

## Will the customer charge change?

The monthly fixed customer charge is proposed to increase from **$14.75 to
$16.10**. This charge recovers the cost of metering, billing, customer service,
and the local distribution facilities that serve the home regardless of how
much electricity is used.

## What about low-income and senior assistance?

The filing **preserves and expands** the Energy Assistance Program (EAP). Income-
qualified households receive a monthly bill credit of up to **$45**. The
qualification threshold is rising from 175% to 200% of the federal poverty
level under the proposal.

## How can I participate?

The Commission welcomes public comment. You can:

- Submit written comments through the Commission's online docket portal,
  referencing the residential rate case docket.
- Attend a public input hearing in person or virtually; dates are posted on
  the Commission's calendar.
- Call the Commission's consumer affairs line.

## Will my service be disconnected if I cannot pay?

No customer is disconnected for non-payment during a declared cold-weather or
heat-emergency period. Payment arrangements are available at any time —
contact the customer service line or visit the utility's payment-help page
online.
