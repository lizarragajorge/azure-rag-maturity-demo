# Substation Arc-Flash PPE Selection Matrix

Aligned with **NFPA 70E-2024 Article 130**. Use this matrix when the
station-specific incident-energy analysis (IEA) is current and on file at the
station entrance. If no IEA is available, default to the highest applicable
category and require a job safety briefing with the area supervisor before
work begins.

## Selection by incident energy at working distance

| Incident energy (cal/cm²) | Category | Minimum PPE                                                                                          |
|---------------------------|----------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| ≤ 1.2                     | n/a      | Long-sleeve FR shirt + FR pants (8 cal/cm² rated). Safety glasses. Hard hat. Voltage-rated gloves.   |
| > 1.2 to ≤ 4              | 1        | Above + arc-rated face shield with balaclava or arc-rated hood. Heavy leather gloves over rubber.    |
| > 4 to ≤ 8                | 2        | Arc-rated coverall or shirt+pants 8 cal/cm². Arc-rated hood. Hearing protection (in-ear).            |
| > 8 to ≤ 25               | 3        | Arc-rated suit 25 cal/cm². Arc-rated flash hood. Hearing protection. Voltage-rated gloves.           |
| > 25 to ≤ 40              | 4        | Arc-rated suit 40 cal/cm². Arc-rated flash hood. Hearing protection. Two-person rule mandatory.      |
| > 40                      | n/a      | **De-energize.** No PPE alone is acceptable; isolation, lockout, and verification required.          |

## Working distance

Default working distances when not otherwise specified by the IEA:

- 15 kV switchgear: **36 inches**
- 25 kV switchgear: **36 inches**
- 5 kV switchgear: **18 inches**
- Low-voltage panelboards (< 1000 V): **18 inches**

A worker who must approach closer than the assumed working distance shall
recompute the incident energy or step up one PPE category.

## Hand protection

Voltage-rated rubber insulating gloves shall be worn whenever the worker is
within the **Restricted Approach Boundary** of an exposed energized conductor,
regardless of arc-flash category. Gloves are tested in-house every 6 months
(ASTM F496) and stamped with the next-test date.

## Audit

Each station's IEA is reviewed within 5 years of issuance or whenever a major
configuration change occurs (new transformer, breaker addition, source-impedance
change in the connected grid). The current-version stamped IEA cover sheet
shall be posted at the station entrance gate.
