# Transmission ROW Vegetation Management Specification

Applies to all transmission lines operated at **200 kV and above**, and
selected 100–200 kV lines designated as critical by the Reliability
Coordinator. Aligned with **NERC FAC-003-5** and the company's Transmission
Vegetation Management Program (TVMP).

## Inspection cadence

| Line class                         | Patrol interval | Method                                |
|------------------------------------|-----------------|---------------------------------------|
| 345 kV transmission                | Annual          | Aerial (helicopter or fixed-wing UAV) |
| 230 kV transmission                | Annual          | Aerial; ground in rugged terrain      |
| 138 kV transmission                | Annual          | Aerial or ground                      |
| Selected 100–138 kV (RC-designated)| Annual          | Aerial or ground                      |
| Critical area (urban canyons, fire zones) | Semi-annual | Ground                              |

LIDAR overflight of every 230 kV+ corridor on a **3-year rolling cycle**
provides the engineering input for clearance modeling.

## Clearance requirements

Minimum vegetation clearance distances (MVCD) are computed for each circuit
at maximum conductor operating temperature and worst-case sag, per IEEE 516
and the Gallet equation. Field crews work to the **clearance-to-bird** distance
published on the per-line specification sheet, which adds a margin above the
MVCD for vegetation growth between cycles.

## Danger trees

A **danger tree** is defined as any tree outside the ROW that, in its
present condition, could contact or fall within the MVCD if it were to fall
toward the line. Danger trees are documented during every patrol with GPS
coordinates, species, height, diameter, and a removal-priority code:

- **Priority A (≤ 30 days):** dead, dying, leaning toward the line, or with
  visible decay.
- **Priority B (≤ 12 months):** structurally compromised but stable at the
  time of patrol.
- **Priority C (next cycle):** identified for monitoring, no immediate
  intervention required.

## Landowner coordination

The company maintains negotiated vegetation easements on the majority of its
ROW. Where easement language is absent or ambiguous, the field forester
attempts written landowner consent before removal of any tree larger than 6
inches DBH. Refusal escalates to the legal department; the company will not
proceed unilaterally except for **Priority A removals**, where the
public-safety and reliability exception in the easement (and FAC-003) is
invoked with documentation.

## Audit

The TVMP and patrol records are retained for the **current FAC-003 audit
cycle plus 10 years**. NERC may sample patrol records, work orders,
photographs, and removal documentation during the standard 14-month audit
window.
