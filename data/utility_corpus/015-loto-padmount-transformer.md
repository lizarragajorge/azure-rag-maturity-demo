# Lockout/Tagout for Pad-Mount Transformers

Applies whenever work is performed on the secondary terminals, internal
components, or grounded enclosure of a pad-mount distribution transformer
where the worker is exposed to energy that would otherwise be present in
normal operation.

## Six steps (per OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147 adapted for utility distribution)

1. **Notify** affected customers and the dispatch operator. Outages are
   coordinated; surprise outages cause escalations and damage trust.
2. **Identify** all sources of energy entering the device: the primary
   loop or radial feed, the secondary service to the customer, any
   parallel/backup feed.
3. **De-energize** by opening the primary elbows (load-break under no-load
   condition where possible) and the secondary breaker or fused disconnect.
4. **Lock and tag** each opened device with the worker's personal padlock
   and tag bearing the worker's name, employee ID, and date. Multi-worker
   jobs use a hasp; **every** worker applies an individual lock.
5. **Verify** the absence of voltage at the work point using a known-good
   voltage detector. Perform a **live-dead-live** check: confirm the detector
   reads voltage on a known-energized source, then reads zero at the work
   point, then reads voltage again on the known source. This proves the
   detector did not fail between the energized check and the work-point check.
6. **Ground** the de-energized conductors with personal protective grounds
   sized for the available fault current. Grounds remain in place for the
   duration of the work.

## Single-worker rule

If a worker is alone, that worker applies their own lock and tag. A worker
may not rely on another person's lock — even a supervisor's — to protect
their own work.

## Lock removal

Only the worker who applied a lock may remove it. The exception, exercised
**only when the worker is unreachable**, requires:

1. Documented attempts to contact the worker.
2. Supervisor approval in writing (electronic ticket is acceptable).
3. A second qualified person to verify the absence of energy and the absence
   of work-in-progress at the device.
4. Notification of the original worker before they return to the site.

## Re-energization

After all locks are removed, the supervisor performs a final walk-through:

- All workers physically clear of the equipment.
- All tools, grounds, and test equipment removed.
- The enclosure is closed and the tamper-resistant seal is reapplied.
- Primary elbows are restored to operating position.
- A test of secondary voltage at the meter base confirms restoration.

The supervisor then notifies dispatch and the affected customer that service
is restored.
