# Cold-Weather Operations — Load Shedding and Firm-Gas Curtailment

This plan covers the integrated electric and gas system response when extreme
cold creates a simultaneous peak in electric heating load and gas distribution
demand. The plan was rebuilt after the **February 2021 Winter Storm Uri**
review and is exercised twice yearly with the Reliability Coordinator.

## Electric system — Energy Emergency Alerts

The Reliability Coordinator (SPP) declares progressively more serious
conditions:

- **EEA Watch:** projected reserves below the operating reserve requirement
  within the next 6 hours.
- **EEA 1:** all available resources committed; voluntary conservation
  requested.
- **EEA 2:** operating reserves cannot be maintained; non-firm load curtailed;
  public conservation appeal escalated through media.
- **EEA 3:** firm load shed is imminent or in progress.

Each step has a documented set of company actions; the EEA 3 actions include
**manual load shed in blocks of 50 MW**, rotated at 30-minute intervals,
following the Distribution Operations Load Shed Plan (NGU-EMR-LOAD-014).

## Firm load shed — distribution feeder selection

Feeders are pre-classified into four blocks. Critical-load feeders (hospitals,
911 centers, water/wastewater pumping, large gas distribution stations) are
**excluded** and rotated last only after exhausting all other options.

| Block | Description                                | Typical priority for shed |
|-------|--------------------------------------------|---------------------------|
| 1     | Predominantly commercial / industrial      | First                     |
| 2     | Mixed urban residential                    | Second                    |
| 3     | Rural residential                          | Third                     |
| 4     | Critical-load (carved out)                 | Last resort               |

A feeder is not held in the shed state longer than **60 minutes** without
explicit dispatch supervisor authorization.

## Gas system — firm vs. interruptible

During a Critical Day declaration:

1. **Interruptible** customers (Schedule IT) are curtailed first, in reverse
   order of contractual priority.
2. **Large-volume firm** customers (Schedule FT-L) receive a curtailment
   warning at 24-hour and 6-hour intervals before a curtailment is implemented.
3. **Residential and small-commercial firm** load is **not curtailed**. If
   distribution pressure cannot otherwise be maintained, the company will
   isolate portions of the distribution system using emergency valves and
   coordinate a planned restoration relight process.

A relight following a system isolation requires a **door-to-door visit** to
every affected service to verify that gas appliances are safely off before
re-pressurization. This process commonly takes 5–10 days for a city-sized
isolation and is the principal reason isolations are avoided whenever possible.

## Coordination with electric load shed

The gas distribution operator informs the electric distribution operator of
any pipeline that is approaching low-pressure conditions; electric feeders
that supply pipeline compressor stations or large gas system pumps are added
to the **electric exclusion list** for the duration of the emergency.
