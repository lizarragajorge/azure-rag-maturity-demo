# Natural Gas Odorization Requirements

Per **49 CFR §192.625**, all natural gas distributed in a class 3 or class 4
location, or in any place where gas may be ignited and present a hazard, must
be odorized so that a person with a normal sense of smell can detect the gas
at a concentration of **one-fifth of the lower explosive limit (1% gas in air
at sea level)**.

## Odorant specification

The utility uses a tertiary-butyl mercaptan / methyl ethyl sulfide
blend (Spotleak 1007 or equivalent) injected at the city gate stations on the
distribution feeds, with a target concentration of **0.5 lb per million
standard cubic feet (lb/MMscf)** and a control band of 0.4 to 0.7 lb/MMscf.

## Injection equipment

- Wick-type odorizers are deprecated and are no longer installed on new
  stations; existing wick units are scheduled for replacement on the regular
  station refresh cycle.
- New installations use **bypass-type liquid-injection odorizers** with PLC
  control proportional to measured throughput.
- A backup pump assembly is required at all stations supplying more than
  500 MMscf/year.

## Verification — quantitative

A **stain-tube reading or chromatographic measurement** of odorant
concentration is performed at each downstream sample point at least quarterly
and within 24 hours of any unplanned odorant interruption. Records are
retained for **5 years**.

## Verification — qualitative ("sniff test")

A monthly sniff test is performed at the **farthest downstream main extremity**
of each odorization zone. Two qualified personnel must independently confirm
detectable odorant at the test stub. Disagreement triggers a quantitative
follow-up within 24 hours.

## Loss of odorization event

If odorant injection is interrupted for more than **15 minutes** during
positive flow, or if quantitative measurement indicates concentration below
the 0.4 lb/MMscf floor at any sample point, the event is classified as a Loss
of Odorization. Required actions:

1. Immediate dispatch of a technician to restore injection.
2. Notification to the gas dispatch supervisor and the on-call compliance
   officer.
3. Field verification at the next downstream sample point within 4 hours.
4. Written event report within 5 business days, retained for the life of the
   pipeline plus 5 years.

A loss-of-odorization event in a class 3 or class 4 location requires
**telephonic notification to the National Response Center** within 1 hour and
a follow-up incident report (Form 7100.1) within 30 days.
